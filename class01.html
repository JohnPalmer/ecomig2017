Logarithms

  • The logarithm of a positive real number x with respect to base b, a positive real number not equal to 1, is the exponent by which b must be raised to yield x. In other words, the logarithm of x to base b is the solution y to the equation:
$$b^{y}=x$$
$$\log_{2}(2) = 1$$
$$\log_{2}(16) = 4$$
$$\log_{10}(100) = 2$$
$$\log_{10}(1) = 0$$
$$\log_{10}\left(\frac{1}{10}\right) = -1$$
$$\log_{4}(2) = .5$$
  • When the base is not specified, the default in the social sciences is usually Euler's number, e:
$$\log(e)=1$$
  • This is known as the natural logarithm, and it is sometimes written as:
$$\ln(e)=1$$
$$e = 2.71828...$$
$$\log\left[\frac{a}{b}\right]=\log a - \log b$$
$$\log ab = \log a + \log b $$
